## 1. DEAL FINDING AND SOURCING

### Where to Find Off-Market Micro Resort Deals
From the challenge curriculum (Module 3):
- **Direct outreach** to hotel owners (cold calling, letters)
- **Broker relationships** -- building and maintaining a broker network
- **Sourcing systems** -- repeatable processes for deal flow
- Properties that "never hit the MLS"

### Outreach Framework
From the challenge page and roadmap materials:
- Week 1 action: "Call 10 hotel owners across your 3 markets (introduce yourself; gauge openness to conversation)"
- The emphasis is on **relationship-first conversations**, not hard pitches
- Start small-scale, share intent/journey, no offering initially
- Build a simple investor tracker (CRM/spreadsheet) from day one

### Deal Flow Tracker
Referenced as a resource: "WK 1: Deal Flow Tracker" (Google Sheet in Accelerator Resources)

### Key Principle
> "Buy poorly operated assets to unlock NOI quickly."

Target properties where current operators are underperforming -- the gap between current NOI and stabilized NOI is where the value-add lives.

## 2. THE BUY BOX FRAMEWORK

### What a Buy Box Contains
From the challenge curriculum (Module 1) -- defining your buy box means locking in:
- **Market** selection criteria
- **Property type** (boutique hotel, micro resort, cabins, glamping, etc.)
- **Deal size** (purchase price range)
- **Return targets** (IRR, Cash-on-Cash, cap rate)

### Gideon's Buy Box Parameters (from roadmaps)
- **First deal target**: $2M-$5M purchase price
- **Day-one NOI required** -- no speculative ground-up builds for first deal
- **Cash-flowing boutique hotels or micro resorts**
- **Non-seasonal** markets preferred
- **Drive-to from a major metro** (1.5 hours or less)
- **Affluent secondary/tertiary markets** -- higher ADR potential at lower entry costs
- **Strong fundamentals**: 10+ years of RevPAR growth, recession resilience
- **Avoid ultra-rural spots**: Easy to buy but hard to sell and scale

### Buy Box Worksheet
A separate worksheet exists for members to build their own buy box (Google Doc ID preserved below). The personalized roadmaps show this is customized per member based on their:
- Goal (income replacement vs. wealth building vs. hybrid)
- Timeline (exploring, 1-2 years, aggressive)
- Capital available
- Involvement level (hands-on, semi-active, passive)
- Strengths and weaknesses
- Funding approach

## 3. MARKET SCREENING AND SELECTION

### Market Screening Toolkit
From personalized roadmaps -- the standard criteria for selecting markets:

| Criterion | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Non-seasonal** | Steadies NOI, reduces cash flow swings | Minimizes volatility for semi-active role and income bridge |
| **Drive-to metro (1.5 hrs or less)** | Taps resilient short-stay demand without urban premiums | Ensures demand resilience; easy for oversight visits |
| **Affluent secondary/tertiary** | Higher ADR potential at lower entry costs | Fits $2-5M guardrail; value plays can reposition for IRR upside |
| **Recession resilience** | Validated via 2008 performance data | De-risks capital-raising pitches |
| **Strong fundamentals** | 10-yr RevPAR growth confirmed in CoStar | Confirms growth trajectory; validates underwriting |
| **Zoning/permitting clarity** | Ability to add 2-6 units (ADUs/casitas) | Optional lever for hybrid CoC/IRR boost |

### Data Sources
- **CoStar** (primary) -- comps, RevPAR trends, property-level data
- **STR reports** (supplemental)
- **AirDNA** (micro-resort context)
- **Local portals** (permitting/zoning)

### Key Insight
> "Underwriting credibility -- and your ability to raise capital -- depends on a professional underwriting model supported by reliable data such as CoStar."

## 4. UNDERWRITING AND DEAL ANALYSIS

### Core Framework
From the challenge curriculum (Module 2):
- **RevPAR modeling** -- Revenue Per Available Room analysis
- **ADR benchmarking** -- Average Daily Rate comparison against comps
- **Pro forma construction** -- building forward-looking financial projections

### Key Metrics and Targets
From personalized roadmaps:
- **Target IRR**: 18%+ over 5-7 years
- **Target Cash-on-Cash (CoC)**: 15%+
- **Target DSCR**: >1.35x (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)
- **Target LTV**: 70% debt
- **Cap rate for purchase**: 8-8.5% (entry); 7-7.5% (exit, compressed)
- **Acquisition fee**: 3-5% of deal (targeting 4-5% for $2-5M properties)

### Underwriting Model
Referenced as an Excel tool: "Underwriting Model" and "Deal Underwriting Model" (multiple versions tracked in Google Drive). This is the core tool members use and is referenced as essential for capital-raising credibility.

### Property Management Fee Strategy
> "Boost CoC by bringing property management in-house, capturing ~7% of gross revenue (underwrite as a PM fee to yourself, not an asset-management fee)."

### Exit Strategy Thinking
- Model multiple exit scenarios from day one: sale, refinance
- "Think multiple exits (sale, refi) from day one."
- Exit in 5 years at compressed cap rate for wealth creation

## 5. FINANCING STRUCTURES

### Financing Methods Taught
From the challenge curriculum (Module 7):
- **DSCR loans** -- Debt Service Coverage Ratio loans (qualify on property income, not personal W-2)
- **SBA 7(a) loans** -- Small Business Administration lending
- **Seller carry-backs** -- seller finances part of the purchase
- **JV equity** -- Joint venture equity partnerships
- **Interest-only (IO) periods** -- reduce risk during learning/stabilization phase

### Capital Stack Framework
From personalized roadmaps:
- **Target**: 70% LTV debt with DSCR >1.35x
- **Clean GP/LP structures** with investor-aligned terms (prefs, promotes)
- **Creative options**: seller financing, carrybacks, layered equity
- **Term sheets and stack design are deal-by-deal; require expert implementation**

### Financing Strategy by Capital Level
- **$0-15k capital**: Lean into sweat equity; attach to experienced team; role = marketing and investor outreach to earn share of fees
- **$15k-50k capital**: Allocate ~$15k toward relationships, tools, education; reserve rest for EMD (Earnest Money Deposits, returned at closing)
- **Higher capital**: More options for GP equity contribution

### Key Principle
> "Finance a micro resort without your W-2 income as the primary qualifier."

This is the core pain point for STR investors trying to scale -- DSCR loans solve it by qualifying on property cash flow.

## 6. CAPITAL STRATEGY AND RAISING CAPITAL

### Acquisition Fee Structure
- **Target fee**: 5% on the deal (range: 3-5%, aiming 4-5% for $2-5M properties)
- **Example**: 5% on a $3M deal = $150k fee; if split two ways, $75k to you at closing
- "This fee becomes your primary cash event to bridge income needs"

### Investor Relations Approach
- Start with light, compliant investor conversations (share intent/journey; no offering)
- Build a simple investor tracker (CRM/spreadsheet)
- Marketing strengths are gold for investor talks, but pair with underwriting to build trust
- Compliance basics must be built early, especially for raising

### Capital Raising Challenges Acknowledged
- Self-limiting beliefs around capital raising
- Need for professional underwriting model to build credibility
- Requires compliance systems for regulatory requirements

## 9. VALUE-ADD AND OPERATIONS STRATEGY

### Value-Add Levers (Skip F&B for First Deal)
From personalized roadmaps -- ranked by complexity and ROI:

1. **Operational tweaks** -- staffing, vendors, SOPs to unlock NOI quickly
2. **Revenue management** -- dynamic pricing, channel mix, direct bookings
3. **Light renovations** -- cosmetic, high-ROI improvements for guest appeal
4. **Adding 2-6 units** -- ADUs/casitas at ~$25k NOI each to boost revenue without full rebuilds (hybrid CoC/IRR play)
5. **Rebrand/reposition** -- story, design, guest experience to drive ADR
6. **Acquire poorly operated assets** -- stabilize NOI fast, leaning on partners for execution

### Property Strategy Core Principle
> "Focus on cash-flowing boutique hotels or micro-resorts with day-one net operating income (NOI) for your first deal -- avoid speculative ground-up projects."

### Operations Goal
- Aim to double property value in 3-5 years via ops execution and light value-add
- Semi-active involvement: oversee a GM/management team
- "Getting the best of cash flow and appreciation without heavy hands-on work"

## 10. TEAM BUILDING AND PARTNERSHIPS

### Recommended Partner Roles (Clear Responsibilities)

| Role | Function | Why Critical |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Analyst / Underwriting Specialist** | Builds/defends the underwriting model; pressure-tests CoStar data and assumptions | Fills analysis gaps; essential for investor confidence |
| **Capital Connector** | Structures investor outreach compliantly; scales raising efforts | Tackles capital-raising gap; leverages marketing to warm leads |
| **Operator** | Leads post-close transition and stabilization | Handles day-to-day; protects semi-active oversight role |
| **Financing / Structuring Advisor** | Optimizes debt terms, seller-carry, DSCR structures | Supports low-capital creative capital stacks |

### Where to Find Partners
- Entry-level: meetups, Facebook (inconsistent but accessible)
- Targeted outreach via personal brand through hospitality networks
- Communities for expert concentration and sustained engagement

### Key Principle
> "Because timelines are often moderately aggressive, capture high-leverage partners first (analysis, capital, ops) to move fast and de-risk."

## 11. THE 30-DAY AND 90-DAY ACTION PLANS

### 30-Day Action Plan (from Personalized Roadmaps)

**Week 1:**
- Select 3 markets using CoStar (non-seasonal, affluent, drive-to, RevPAR trend)
- Build a simple investor tracker (CRM/spreadsheet)
- Connect with an experienced underwriter and schedule time
- Call 10 hotel owners across your 3 markets (introduce yourself; gauge openness)

**Week 2:**
- Pull property-level CoStar data for candidate assets
- Review 1-2 deals with your underwriter (focus on inputs/assumptions)
- Start light, compliant investor conversations (share intent/journey; no offering)

**Week 3:**
- Review 1-2 more deals with your underwriter
- Begin outreach to potential partners (Analyst, Capital Connector, Operator, Structurer)
- Keep updating your investor tracker

**Week 4:**
- Reach 4 total deals reviewed with expert validation
- Narrow to 1-2 markets based on fundamentals + deal flow
- Check investor alignment with your thesis (adjust positioning if needed)
- Submit an LOI on the most promising property

## 14. KEY TERMINOLOGY GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **ADR** | Average Daily Rate -- the average rental income per paid occupied room per day |
| **RevPAR** | Revenue Per Available Room -- total room revenue divided by total available rooms |
| **NOI** | Net Operating Income -- revenue minus operating expenses (before debt service) |
| **DSCR** | Debt Service Coverage Ratio -- NOI divided by annual debt service; lenders want >1.35x |
| **LTV** | Loan-to-Value ratio -- loan amount as percentage of property value; target 70% |
| **CoC / Cash-on-Cash** | Annual pre-tax cash flow divided by total cash invested; target 15%+ |
| **IRR** | Internal Rate of Return -- annualized total return including appreciation; target 18%+ |
| **Cap Rate** | Capitalization Rate -- NOI divided by property value; used for valuation |
| **EMD** | Earnest Money Deposit -- good-faith deposit returned at closing |
| **GP/LP** | General Partner / Limited Partner structure for equity raises |
| **LOI** | Letter of Intent -- non-binding offer document |
| **PSA** | Purchase and Sale Agreement -- binding contract after LOI acceptance |
| **SBA 7(a)** | Small Business Administration loan program for business acquisitions |
| **Seller Carry** | Seller finances a portion of the purchase price |
| **JV Equity** | Joint Venture equity -- partnership capital for deals |
| **IO Period** | Interest-Only period -- reduces payments during stabilization |
| **ADU / Casita** | Accessory Dwelling Unit -- additional small units added to a property |
| **F&B** | Food and Beverage -- restaurant/bar operations (skip for first deal) |
| **PM Fee** | Property Management fee -- ~7% of gross revenue; model to yourself for CoC boost |
| **Buy Box** | Defined investment criteria document specifying target markets, property types, price range, and return targets |
| **Sweat Equity** | Value contributed through work/expertise rather than capital |
| **Value-Add** | Improvements that increase property NOI and value |
| **Stabilized NOI** | NOI after operational improvements are implemented |
| **Pref / Promote** | Preferred return (paid first to LPs) / Promote (GP's share above the pref) |

## 15. WORKING DEAL EXAMPLES

### Example 1: $3.5M Boutique Hotel (from Manny's Roadmap)
- **Purchase price**: $3.5M at 8% cap rate
- **Day-one NOI**: $280k
- **Debt**: 70% LTV ($2.45M loan, 25-yr amortization, 6.5% rate) = ~$198k annual debt service
- **Day-one cash flow after debt**: $280k - $198k = $82k (23% CoC on $350k equity)
- **Acquisition fee**: 5% on $3.5M = $175k; two-way split = $87.5k to you at closing
- **Value-add**: Add 4 units at $25k NOI each = +$100k; stabilized NOI = $380k
- **Stabilized cash flow**: $380k - $198k = $182k (~15%+ CoC target met)
- **Exit in 5 years**: $380k / 7% cap = $5.4M value; ~$1.2M equity created after loan paydown

### Example 2: $3.5M Boutique Hotel, Austin Outskirts (from Marcus's Roadmap)
- **Purchase price**: $3.5M at 8.5% cap rate (affluent secondary market, drive-to from metro)
- **Day-one NOI**: $297,500
- **Debt**: 70% LTV ($2.45M loan, 25-yr amort, 6.5% rate) = $204k annual debt service
- **Day-one cash flow**: $297,500 - $204,000 = $93,500
- **Acquisition fee**: 5% = $175k; two-way split = $87.5k at closing
- **Value-add**: Add 4 units at $25k NOI each = +$100k; new stabilized NOI = $397,500
- **Stabilized cash flow after debt**: $193,500
- **Exit in 5 years**: $397,500 / 7.5% cap = $5.3M; ~$1.2M equity created
- "This illustrates 20% IRR potential"

### Key Takeaways from Both Examples
1. Start with day-one cash flow for stability, then layer value-add for IRR
2. Model PM fee to yourself for CoC turbocharge
3. Validate all assumptions in CoStar for investor buy-in
4. Think multiple exits (sale, refi) from day one
5. Adding units if zoning allows boosts revenue without heavy involvement
6. Always underwrite with CoStar for RevPAR trends
