How We Acquired a 24-Unit Boutique Hotel with Creative Financing

A full walkthrough of the deal structure, capital stack, stabilization playbook, and lessons learned — for operators who are actively buying.

03 — The Capital Stack

This is a three-layer financing structure. We raised $3M from investors, put 20%+ down, and layered in three forms of creative debt to make the numbers work. Here's exactly how it broke down:

Hotel — $4.7M

Private Money Lending

$1.32M @ 5% — 5-year interest-only

Equity Down

$550K @ 3.99% — 30-year fixed (inherited from seller)

$410K @ 5% (yr 1-2), 7% (yr 3-7) — interest-only

$240K

This deal does not pencil without the seller financing. Period. We showed up with $3M raised and 20%+ down — real capital, real credibility — and the numbers still didn't work on conventional terms alone.
04 — Negotiating the Seller Financing

The mistake most buyers make: they treat seller financing as a concession — something you ask for when you can't qualify. We came at it from the opposite direction.

💰

We Showed Up with Capital

$3M raised, 20%+ down. The seller knew we weren't asking because we were broke. We were asking because the math demanded it.

🤝

Framed as Value for the Seller

Seller financing at 5% generates better yield than cash sitting idle. We helped him see it as an investment he was making — not a favor he was doing us.

📐

Walked Through the Math Live

We showed him the deal model. "Without these terms, there is no deal. With them, here's what you earn and here's our exit plan." Transparent beats clever every time.

Favorable terms are not a sign of weakness. They are how you structure a deal that works for everyone at the table. If you can't close without them, say so — and show why the seller should want that too.
05 — Stabilization Playbook

We ran two tracks simultaneously — revenue optimization and capital improvement — sequenced intentionally by season.

Busy Season Strategy

Revenue First, Renovations Later

  • Maximized revenue without touching the asset
  • Implemented dynamic pricing
  • Improved marketing and distribution channels
  • Pushed direct bookings to reduce OTA fees
  • Began group and events pipeline

Slow Season Execution

Capital Improvements

  • Phase 1 (Apr-Sept 2024): Exterior paint, linen refresh, landscaping renovation, ops handoff
  • Phase 2 (Oct 2024-Feb 2025): Full interior renovation, rebrand launch, local partnerships
  • $900K renovation budget with 20% contingency reserves

The GC Management System

Renovation chaos is expensive. We pre-loaded everything before the GC showed up.

🖼

Printed Room Images

Physical printed images of the target finish were taped inside each room. Zero ambiguity about what "done" looks like.

📦

FF&E Organized by Room

Furniture, fixtures, and equipment itemized and labeled per room before a single nail was pulled.

🚛

Everything On-Site Before Start

All product ordered and staged in containers on-site ahead of the work start date. No waiting on deliveries mid-project.

06 — Permitting & Land Use

The duplex (296 1st St) sits adjacent to the permitted hotel in a city actively tightening STR rules. We needed to legitimize its use — and figure out how to navigate a city council that wasn't obviously friendly to the plan.

Old Approach (Pre-AI)

  • Hire a consultant
  • Hope they know the room
  • Guess at how council members feel
  • Show up and find out

What We Did This Time

  • Fed council meeting minutes into AI
  • Identified not just voting likelihood, but sentiment
  • Understood which arguments each member responds to
  • Walked in knowing the temperature of the room
Using AI to analyze council meeting minutes was phenomenal. We didn't just know if they would or wouldn't approve — we understood how people felt about it and what language resonated with each member. That's a legitimate edge.

We engaged two consultants with opposing strategies: Erik (aggressive rezone path — $30-40K, 6-12 months, strong long-term insulation) vs. Heidi Jones at Meraki Land Use Consulting (conservative path — pressure-test with a council member first before spending). The AI analysis helped us evaluate which path to take with actual data instead of guesswork.

07 — Lessons Learned
📋

Hire the Permitting Consultant Earlier

Before AI was available, we would have gone back and hired a consultant earlier in the permitting process. Getting ahead of the entitlement question saves time and money downstream.

🤖

Use AI for Regulatory Intelligence

This is now a standard part of our diligence process. Reading meeting minutes manually doesn't give you the same picture that AI analysis of sentiment patterns does.

08 — Key Takeaways for Active Deal Buyers
01

Structure the deal so it has to work

If the numbers don't work without seller financing, say so transparently — then show the seller why that's good for them too. Credibility + honesty closes more than clever negotiation.

02

Pre-load your renovation before the GC shows up

Chaos during renovation is expensive. Printed images in every room, FF&E organized by room, product staged on-site before day one. Preparation is the margin.

03

AI for regulatory sentiment is a real edge

Analyzing council meeting minutes with AI gives you something no consultant can — an understanding of how people feel, not just how they vote. Use it before you spend on entitlement strategy.
